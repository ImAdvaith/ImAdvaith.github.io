Tenant's Guide FAQs

Is it possible for a foreigner to purchase property in Singapore? +

Yes, foreigners can purchase property in Singapore in designated freehold areas. The Singapore government has designated specific zones where non-SG nationals can buy property with full ownership rights.

Can a foreigner have 100 per cent ownership of property in Singapore? +

Yes, foreigners can have 100% ownership of properties in designated freehold areas in Singapore. These areas include popular locations such as Singapore Marina, Downtown Singapore, Orchad street, and many other developments.

Is it a good idea to invest in real estate in Singapore? +

Investing in Singapore real estate can be profitable due to factors such as tax-free rental income, high rental yields (typically 5-10%), property value appreciation in prime areas, and a stable economy. However, like any investment, it comes with risks and should be approached after thorough research of the market.

Can I get a visa if I own a property in Singapore? +

Yes, property owners in Singapore may be eligible for property investor visas (also known as property residence visas). Generally, the property value must be at least SGD 1 million, and the visa is valid for 2-3 years with renewal options. Requirements and regulations may change, so it's advisable to check with the Singapore Land Department for current rules.

Is it possible to purchase a property in Singapore as a company? +

Yes, both local and foreign companies can purchase property in Singapore's designated freehold areas. This approach may offer tax advantages and asset protection benefits depending on your circumstances. Setting up a company for this purpose typically requires legal guidance to ensure compliance with SG regulations.
